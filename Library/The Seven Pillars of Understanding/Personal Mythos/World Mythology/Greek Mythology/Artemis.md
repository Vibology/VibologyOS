## Overview

**Artemis** (Ἄρτεμις, Roman: **Diana**) is the Olympian goddess of the **wilderness, the hunt, and the moon**. Twin sister to Apollo, she represents the wild, untamed feminine—the aspect of the feminine that refuses domestication, that moves freely through forests, that answers to no man.

Artemis is the **eternal virgin** (*parthenos*)—not in the sense of sexual repression, but as the woman who is *whole unto herself*, who does not define her existence through relationship to masculine authority. She protects young women, presides over childbirth (despite never bearing children herself), and punishes those who violate her sacred boundaries.

---

## Key Myths

**Birth of Artemis and Apollo:**
Artemis was born first and immediately assisted her mother Leto in delivering her twin brother Apollo—establishing her role as **protector of women in childbirth**.

**Actaeon's Punishment:**
When the hunter Actaeon stumbled upon Artemis bathing naked, she transformed him into a stag and his own hounds tore him apart—enforcing the principle that **her body and autonomy are inviolate**.

**The Bear of Brauron:**
Young Athenian girls served Artemis at her sanctuary in Brauron, dancing as "bears" (*arktoi*) in ritual initiations. This encoded the transition from wildness (bear) to civilization (marriageable woman)—yet Artemis herself remained forever wild.

## Archetypal Significance

**The Wild Feminine:**
Artemis represents the **feminine principle that refuses possession**—the aspect of woman that is self-sufficient, fierce, and sovereign. She is not the mother (Demeter), the lover (Aphrodite), or the wife (Hera)—she is the **virgin as autonomous being**.

**Lunar Consciousness:**
Unlike her brother Apollo (solar consciousness, clarity, logic), Artemis embodies **lunar consciousness**: instinctual, cyclical, connected to nature's rhythms. She moves by moonlight, tracking what daylight cannot see.

**Protector and Destroyer:**
Artemis protects the vulnerable (young women, children, animals) but destroys those who violate boundaries. She is fierce compassion—love that will kill to defend what it cherishes.
