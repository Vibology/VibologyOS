# The 30 Aethyrs
*The Celestial Spheres — From TEX to LIL, From Earth to the Throne of God*

---
## Introduction

The **30 Aethyrs** (also called Aires or Ethers) are concentric spheres of consciousness revealed to [[John Dee and Edward Kelley]] as part of the complete [[Enochian System Overview|Enochian system]]. Each Aethyr represents a **distinct level of reality**, ascending from the densest material manifestation (30th Aethyr, TEX) to the highest divine unity (1st Aethyr, LIL).

**Key Characteristics:**

- **Numbered in reverse:** 30 (outermost) to 1 (innermost)
- **Three-letter Enochian names:** Each Aethyr bears a name in the angelic alphabet
- **91 Governors total:** Each Aethyr (except the first three) is governed by three angels
- **Visionary content:** Scrying the Aethyrs reveals archetypal imagery, initiatory trials, and direct revelation
- **Qabalistic parallel:** Often mapped to the ten Sephiroth in triads

The Aethyrs function as a **map of consciousness**—a sequential initiatory journey from ego-identification to transpersonal realization. **Aleister Crowley's** scrying of all 30 Aethyrs in 1909 (published as *The Vision and the Voice*) remains the most comprehensive record of their content.

---
## Structure and Organization

### The Reverse Numbering

Unlike most mystical systems (which ascend from 1 to higher numbers), the Aethyrs descend from **30 to 1**:

- **30 (TEX)** — The outermost sphere, closest to material manifestation
- **1 (LIL)** — The innermost sphere, the throne of divine unity

This reflects the Enochian cosmology: the divine emanates **outward** from unity (LIL) into multiplicity (TEX). The magician's journey **reverses this flow**, ascending from fragmentation back to source.

### The 91 Governors

Each Aethyr from 30 down to 4 is governed by **three angels** (3 × 27 = 81 Governors). The first three Aethyrs (LIL, ARN, ZOM) are governed by **10 angels total** (3 + 3 + 4). Total: **91 Governors**.

**Function of Governors:**
- Mediate the specific consciousness of their Aethyr
- Appear in visions to guide, test, or reveal
- Can be invoked individually for specific purposes

**Names:** The Governor names are derived from the **Great Table** (Watchtowers) through complex letter extraction methods revealed by the angels.

### Qabalistic Mapping (Triadic Attribution)

Many practitioners arrange the Aethyrs in **triads** corresponding to the ten Sephiroth:

| Aethyrs | Sephirah | Stage |
|---------|----------|-------|
| 30-28 (TEX, RII, BAG) | [[Malkuth]] | Material manifestation, ego formation |
| 27-25 (ZAA, DES, VTI) | [[Yesod]] | Dreams, subconscious, lunar consciousness |
| 24-22 (LIN, RII, BAG) | [[Hod]] | Intellect, analysis, communication |
| 21-19 (ASP, ZEN, POP) | [[Netzach]] | Emotion, desire, aesthetic beauty |
| 18-16 (ZEN, LEA, TAN) | [[Tiphareth]] | Integration, Higher Self, solar consciousness |
| 15-13 (OXO, VTA, ZIM) | [[Geburah]] | Strength, discrimination, necessary destruction |
| 12-10 (LOE, ICH, ZAX) | [[Chesed]] to [[Daath]] | Mercy, expansion, and **the Abyss** |
| 9-7 (ZIP, ZID, DEO) | [[Binah]] | Understanding, form, the Great Mother |
| 6-4 (MAZ, LIT, PAZ) | [[Chokmah]] | Wisdom, explosive force, the Logos |
| 3-1 (ZOM, ARN, LIL) | [[Kether]] | Unity, the Crown, dissolution into God |

**The 10th Aethyr (ZAX)** is the most critical threshold—it corresponds to **Daath**, the Abyss, the terrifying void separating human consciousness from divine consciousness.

**Governors:** Taoagla, Gemnimb, Advorpt (some sources add Dozinal as a fourth)

**Qabalistic:** Malkuth (Kingdom, material plane)

**Function:** The foundation of manifestation, the physical world, sensory reality

**Visionary Content (Crowley):** The material plane seen from above; recognition of the prison of form; the beginning of the ascent

**Theme:** Incarnation, embodiment, the density of matter

---

**Governors:** Vastrim, Opmacas, Genadol

**Qabalistic:** Malkuth (with Yesod influence)

**Theme:** Awakening to the possibility of transcendence

### 10th Aethyr: ZAX — **THE ABYSS**

**Governors:** Lexarph, Comanan, Tabitom

**Qabalistic:** **Daath** (the hidden Sephirah, the void between human and divine)

**Function:** **The total annihilation of the ego, the crossing of the Abyss**

**Visionary Content (Crowley):** The demon **Choronzon**, the dweller in the Abyss, who embodies dispersion, chaos, and meaninglessness. Crowley drew a protective circle and invoked Choronzon, who attempted to deceive, seduce, and destroy him. The vision culminates in the recognition that **all form is emptiness**, all meaning is arbitrary, and only by **complete surrender** can the aspirant pass.

**Theme:** Death of the ego, the Dark Night of the Soul, annihilation

**Warning:** This is the most dangerous Aethyr. Many practitioners stop here. Those who proceed unprepared risk psychosis, spiritual inflation, or total disintegration. Only those who have fully integrated their Shadow, surrendered all attachment, and grounded themselves in humility should attempt to cross.

**Crowley's Experience:** He described ZAX as the most harrowing ordeal of his life—worse than the K2 expedition, worse than any physical danger. It is the **initiatory death** that precedes rebirth into the Supernal Triad.

## Practical Application: Scrying the Aethyrs

### Preparation

**Do not approach the Aethyrs casually.** They demand:

1. **Spiritual maturity** — Integration of Shadow, stable ego structure
2. **Magical discipline** — Mastery of banishing, invocation, grounding
3. **Physical health** — Fasting, rest, sobriety (no intoxicants)
4. **Sacred space** — Consecrated working area, protective circle

### Method (Crowley's Approach)

**1. Banishing:**
- Perform the Lesser Banishing Ritual of the Pentagram (LBRP)
- Establish sacred space

**2. Invocation of the Aethyr:**
- Use the **19th Enochian Key (Call of the 30 Aethyrs)**, substituting the name of the desired Aethyr
- Example: "MADRIAAX...TEX..." (for the 30th Aethyr)

**3. Scrying:**
- Gaze into a crystal, shewstone, or enter trance
- Allow visions to arise without forcing or editing
- **Record immediately**—speak aloud or write as the vision unfolds (do not rely on memory)

**4. Integration:**
- After the vision ends, close with banishing
- Rest, ground, eat
- Meditate on the vision over days/weeks, cross-reference with Qabalistic and Tarot symbolism

**5. Sequential Progression:**
- Always work from **30 down to 1** (never skip, never jump to higher Aethyrs)
- Some practitioners spend weeks or months between Aethyrs
- **Do not proceed to the next Aethyr until the current one is fully integrated**

### Safety Protocols

**Never invoke the Aethyrs if:**
- You are mentally unstable, depressed, or in crisis
- You have not mastered basic banishing and grounding techniques
- You are using drugs or alcohol (including psychedelics)
- You are motivated by curiosity rather than genuine spiritual aspiration

**Signs of improper work:**
- Persistent anxiety, paranoia, or delusions after scrying
- Ego inflation ("I am a great magician")
- Loss of grounding in daily life
- Obsessive fixation on visions

**Remedy:** Stop all Aethyr work immediately. Focus on grounding (physical exercise, nature, routine). Seek guidance from experienced practitioners or therapists.

## The Vision and the Voice: Crowley's Legacy

In **1909**, Aleister Crowley scried all 30 Aethyrs in the Algerian desert with his disciple **Victor Neuburg**. The resulting visions were published as ***The Vision and the Voice***, one of the most extraordinary mystical documents of the 20th century.

**Key Contributions:**

**1. Complete Record:** Crowley's visions provide detailed content for each Aethyr, filling in what Dee and Kelley did not record

**2. Thelemic Interpretation:** Crowley mapped the Aethyrs onto his Thelemic cosmology (e.g., Babalon in the 14th, the Crowned and Conquering Child in higher Aethyrs)

**3. The Abyss (ZAX):** Crowley's account of crossing the 10th Aethyr remains the definitive description of the initiatory ordeal

**4. Practical Demonstration:** Crowley showed that the Aethyrs are **accessible**—not abstract theology but lived experience

**Criticism:** Some argue Crowley's Thelemic overlay distorts the original Enochian content. Others see it as a valid evolution, demonstrating the Aethyrs' adaptability across paradigms.

- **The Fool (0)** — The leap from TEX (30) into the unknown

- **ZIP to LIL (9-1)** — Integration of the collective unconscious, union with the transpersonal, realization of the Self

**Active Imagination:** Scrying the Aethyrs resembles Jung's practice of dialoguing with autonomous psychic structures.

## The Shadow of the Aethyrs

### Spiritual Bypassing

**Danger:** Using the Aethyrs to escape mundane responsibility, relationships, or psychological work

**Shadow Manifestation:** "I'm too spiritually advanced for ordinary life," neglecting health, finances, or loved ones

**Remedy:** Ground the visions in daily life. Integration means bringing the transcendent into the immanent, not abandoning embodiment.

### Ego Inflation

**Danger:** Identifying with the visions, believing oneself to be a prophet or chosen one

**Shadow Manifestation:** Grandiosity, arrogance, dismissal of others as "unenlightened"

**Remedy:** Recognize that visions are **experiences of the Self**, not the ego. Humility is the mark of true initiation.

### Psychic Destabilization

**Danger:** Opening to forces beyond the ego's capacity to integrate

**Shadow Manifestation:** Anxiety, paranoia, hallucinations persisting after scrying

**Remedy:** Stop all magical work. Seek grounding practices, therapy, or experienced guidance. The Aethyrs will wait.

## The Gift of the Aethyrs

At their highest expression, the 30 Aethyrs offer:

**A map of consciousness** — From material density to divine unity
**An initiatory ladder** — Sequential stages of transformation
**Direct revelation** — Contact with autonomous transpersonal intelligences
**The dissolution of illusion** — Stripping away all false constructs
**Union with the divine** — The return to source, the completion of the Great Work

The Aethyrs are not a doctrine to believe but a **journey to undertake**. They do not promise comfort or certainty—they promise **truth**. Those who enter with courage, humility, and discipline will find that the path from TEX to LIL is the path of the soul returning home.
