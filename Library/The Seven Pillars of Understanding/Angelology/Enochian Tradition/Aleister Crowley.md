## Overview

**Aleister Crowley** (1875-1947) was an English occultist, ceremonial magician, poet, painter, novelist, and mountaineer. He founded the religious philosophy of **Thelema**, identifying himself as the prophet entrusted with guiding humanity into the Aeon of Horus.

For the [[Enochian Tradition]], Crowley's significance lies in his **practical validation** of the system received by [[John Dee and Edward Kelley]]. In 1909, in the Algerian desert, Crowley scried all [[The 30 Aethyrs]], producing *The Vision and the Voice*—the most detailed record of Aethyric experience ever published.

## Life Overview

**Born:** October 12, 1875, Leamington Spa, England
**Died:** December 1, 1947, Hastings, England

### Key Events

- **1898:** Initiated into [[The Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn]]
- **1900:** Received Adeptus Minor grade; conflict with Mathers began
- **1904:** Received *The Book of the Law* in Cairo (founding text of Thelema)
- **1909:** Scried all 30 Aethyrs in Algeria with Victor Neuburg
- **1910:** Published *The Vision and the Voice*
- **1920s-30s:** Led the A∴A∴ and Ordo Templi Orientis (O.T.O.)
- **1947:** Died in relative obscurity, later recognized as influential figure

## Contribution to Enochian Magic

### The Vision and the Voice (1909)

Crowley's scrying of the 30 Aethyrs demonstrated that the Enochian system **works**—it produces genuine visionary content, not mere fantasy.

**Key contributions:**
- **Detailed records** of each Aethyr's imagery, entities, and teachings
- **The Abyss crossing** (10th Aethyr, ZAX) — Encounter with Choronzon, the demon of dispersion
- **Thelemic interpretation** — Integration of Enochian with the new Aeon
- **Babalon** — The goddess figure encountered in the 14th Aethyr

### Practical Methodology

Crowley used the **19th Key** (Call of the 30 Aethyrs) with modifications:
- Performed in remote desert location for isolation
- Victor Neuburg served as scribe and magical assistant
- Each Aethyr scried in sequence over several weeks

## Thelema

Crowley's religious philosophy, summarized:

- **"Do what thou wilt shall be the whole of the Law"** — Discover and fulfill your True Will
- **"Love is the law, love under will"** — Will directed by love
- **The Aeon of Horus** — A new era of self-realization replacing the Aeon of Osiris (Christianity)
