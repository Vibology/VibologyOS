# Enochian Tradition
*The Angelic Alphabet, the Watchtowers of Creation, and the 30 Aethyrs of Consciousness*

---
## Essence
The **Enochian system** is a comprehensive magical and angelological framework revealed to **Dr. John Dee** (1527-1608/9) and **Edward Kelley** (1555-1597) through intensive scrying sessions conducted between 1581 and 1585. Unlike the Pseudo-Dionysian hierarchy (which emerged from theological contemplation) or the Qabalistic attributions (which developed through centuries of mystical tradition), the Enochian system claims **direct angelic revelation**—a complete cosmology, language, hierarchy, and magical technology dictated by celestial intelligences.

The system encompasses:

- **The Enochian Language** — A complete alphabet, grammar, and vocabulary revealed as the "language of angels" or the original Adamic tongue
- **The Heptarchia Mystica** — A system of 49 Good Angels governing planetary forces, invoked through the Sigillum Dei Aemeth
- **The Great Table (Watchtowers)** — Four elemental tablets containing hierarchies of angels, divine names, and sub-angelic intelligences
- **The 30 Aethyrs** — Concentric spheres of consciousness ascending from earthly manifestation (TEX) to divine unity (LIL)
- **The 48 Enochian Keys (Calls)** — Invocations in the Enochian language that activate specific angelic forces
- **Liber Loagaeth** — 49 tables of mystical letter-grids revealed as the "Book of the Speech from God"

## Historical Context: Dee, Kelley, and the Angels

### John Dee: The Queen's Magus

**Dr. John Dee** was no mere occultist—he was mathematician, astronomer, cartographer, and advisor to Queen Elizabeth I. His library at Mortlake contained over 4,000 volumes, the largest private collection in England. Dee approached angelic communication not as superstition but as **natural philosophy**—a rigorous investigation into the hidden structure of reality.

Dee's motivations were:
- **Scientific:** To receive hidden knowledge about the cosmos
- **Theological:** To restore the original language spoken by Adam before Babel
- **Political:** To receive guidance for England's role in divine providence
- **Mystical:** To achieve communion with angelic intelligences

### Edward Kelley: The Scryer

**Edward Kelley** (also spelled Kelly) served as Dee's scryer—the clairvoyant medium who gazed into the "shewstone" (a polished obsidian mirror or crystal) and reported the visions. While Kelley's character remains controversial (some sources claim he was a charlatan, others a genuine mystic), the **internal consistency and sophistication** of the received material suggests something beyond simple fraud.

### The Angelic Sessions (1581-1585)

The revelations unfolded over four years:

**1581-1582:** Early visions, appearance of angelic intermediaries ([[Uriel]], Ave, Nalvage)
**1582:** Revelation of the Heptarchia Mystica and Sigillum Dei Aemeth
**1583-1584:** Reception of Liber Loagaeth (49 mystical tables)
**1584-1585:** Revelation of the Great Table (Watchtowers), the 48 Calls, and the 30 Aethyrs

The angels insisted that this knowledge was being restored to humanity after millennia of loss—that Enochian was the language used by the patriarch **Enoch** before his ascension.[^1]

## The Three Core Systems

### 1. Heptarchia Mystica

**Structure:** 49 Good Angels organized by the seven classical planets, invoked through the **Sigillum Dei Aemeth** (Seal of God's Truth)

**Function:** Planetary magic, petition for knowledge, worldly power, and divine favor

**Key Components:**
- **The Sigillum Dei Aemeth** — A complex wax seal inscribed with divine names, serving as the foundation for angelic invocation
- **The Table of Practice** — A consecrated table upon which the Sigillum is placed
- **The Holy Table** — A larger table supporting the scrying equipment
- **49 Angels** — Seven sets of seven angels, each governing a planetary force and day of the week

**Primary Angels:**
- **King Carmara** (Sunday/Sun), **King Hagonel** (Monday/Moon), **King Baligon** (Tuesday/Mars), **King Babalel** (Wednesday/Mercury), **King Bornogo** (Thursday/Jupiter), **King Bnapsen** (Friday/Venus), **King Bnaspol** (Saturday/Saturn)

### 2. The Great Table (Watchtowers)

**Structure:** Four elemental tablets (Air/East, Water/West, Fire/South, Earth/North) plus the central **Tablet of Union**, forming a unified grid of divine and angelic names

**Function:** Elemental magic, directional invocation, manifestation work, cosmological mapping

**Each Watchtower contains:**
- **One King** (ruling the entire quadrant)
- **Six Seniors** (governing sub-elements and planets)
- **16 Sub-Angles** (lesser angelic hierarchies)
- **Kerubic Squares** (four corner angels)
- **Servient Squares** (numerous lesser entities)
- **Calvary Cross** (central divine names)

**The Four Kings:**
- **Bataivah** (Air/East)
- **Raagiosl** (Water/West)
- **Edlprna** (Fire/South)
- **Iczhhcal** (Earth/North)

**The Tablet of Union:** Five lines of divine names that unify the four Watchtowers, invoked before any elemental working

### 3. The 30 Aethyrs

**Structure:** Concentric spheres of consciousness, numbered from **30 (TEX)** at the outermost (closest to material manifestation) to **1 (LIL)** at the innermost (nearest to divine source)

**Function:** Visionary ascent, initiatory pathworking, consciousness expansion, mystical revelation

**Each Aethyr:**
- Bears a **three-letter Enochian name**
- Is governed by **three Governors** (91 total across all 30 Aethyrs)
- Contains specific visionary content, archetypal imagery, and spiritual trials
- Corresponds to stages of mystical ascent analogous to the Qabalistic Tree of Life

**Aleister Crowley's Contribution:** In 1909, Crowley scried all 30 Aethyrs in the Algerian desert, publishing the visions in *The Vision and the Voice*. His accounts remain the most detailed record of Aethyric content, blending Dee's framework with Thelemic interpretation.

## The Enochian Language

### Structure and Characteristics

**The Enochian alphabet** consists of 21 letters, each with a corresponding name, phonetic value, and numerical attribution. The language exhibits:

- **Consistent grammar** — Subject-verb-object structure, conjugations, declensions
- **Unique vocabulary** — Over 600 words revealed through the angelic sessions
- **Phonetic beauty** — Described by practitioners as "numinous," "resonant," and "vibratory"
- **Reversibility** — Enochian Keys are often spoken in reverse order (from 18th to 1st) to invoke forces, then forward (1st to 18th) to banish

### The 48 Enochian Keys (Calls)

The **Keys** are invocations in the Enochian language, each activating specific angelic forces or opening specific Aethyrs and are structured as follows:

- **Calls 1-2:** General invocations establishing sacred space and divine authority
- **Calls 3-18:** Open the 30 Aethyrs (Call 3 opens 30th, 29th, 28th Aethyr; Call 4 opens 27th, 26th, 25th, etc.)
- **Call 19:** The "Call of the 30 Aethyrs" (used to invoke any specific Aethyr)
- **Calls 20-27, 29-30:** Not revealed or lost
- **Call 31-48:** Govern specific sub-angles of the Watchtowers

**Example (Second Call, opening lines):**
*Enochian:* "Adgt upaah zong om faaip sald, vi-i-v L sobam ial-prt..."
*English:* "Can the wings of the winds understand your voices of wonder, O you the second of the first..."

## Qabalistic Integration

Though revealed independently, the Enochian system has been extensively mapped onto the **Qabalistic Tree of Life** by later practitioners (especially the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn and Aleister Crowley):

### The 30 Aethyrs and the Tree

The Aethyrs are often arranged in **triads** corresponding to the ten Sephiroth:

- **LIL, ARN, ZOM** (1-3) → Kether
- **PAZ, LIT, MAZ** (4-6) → Chokmah
- **DEO, ZID, ZIP** (7-9) → Binah
- **ZAX, ICH, LOE** (10-12) → Daath (the Abyss)
- **ZIM, VTA, OXO** (13-15) → Chesed
- **LEA, TAN, TOR** (16-18) → Geburah
- **ASP, ZEN, POP** (19-21) → Tiphareth
- **LIN, RII, BAG** (22-24) → Netzach
- **VTI, DES, ZAA** (25-27) → Hod
- **BAG, RII, LIN** (28-30) → Yesod/Malkuth

**Critical Threshold:** The **10th Aethyr (ZAX)** corresponds to **Daath** (the Abyss)—the terrifying void separating the human triad from the supernal triad. Crowley's vision of ZAX remains one of the most harrowing accounts in mystical literature.

### Watchtowers and Elements

The four Watchtowers correspond to:
- **Air (East)** — [[Raphael]], Swords, intellect, communication
- **Water (West)** — [[Gabriel]], Cups, emotion, intuition
- **Fire (South)** — [[Michael]], Wands, will, creative force
- **Earth (North)** — [[Uriel]], Pentacles, manifestation, embodiment

Each Watchtower's **Six Seniors** are attributed to the seven classical planets (excluding one per element):
- Fire excludes Luna (Moon)
- Earth excludes Venus
- Air excludes Jupiter
- Water excludes Mercury

## Correspondences with Other Systems

### Tarot

**Court Cards** map to the **16 sub-angles** of each Watchtower:
- **Kings** — Kerubic (Fire of Element)
- **Queens** — Angelic (Water of Element)
- **Princes** — Ruling (Air of Element)
- **Princesses** — Servient (Earth of Element)

**Major Arcana:** Some practitioners map the 22 Major Arcana to paths between Aethyrs or to specific Governors, though this remains speculative.

### Astrology

**Zodiacal attributions** appear in the Watchtowers:
- Each sub-angle governs 10° of a zodiacal sign
- The Seniors correspond to planetary forces
- The Great Table as a whole maps the zodiac across four quadrants

### Human Design

No direct correspondence exists, though practitioners might map:
- **Throat Center** to Air Watchtower (communication, manifestation)
- **Solar Plexus** to Water Watchtower (emotional wave)
- **Sacral** to Fire Watchtower (creative life force)
- **Root** to Earth Watchtower (pressure, grounding)

## Invocation
### Invocation of the Watchtowers

**Standard Opening Sequence:**
- **Lesser Banishing Ritual of the Pentagram (LBRP)** — Establish sacred space
- **Invoke Tablet of Union** — "Exarp, Hcoma, Nanta, Bitom" (unifying divine names)
- **Invoke Four Watchtowers** — Call the Kings and Seniors of each direction
- **Specific Work** — Petition angels for knowledge, power, or vision
- **License to Depart** — Thank and release the forces
- **Closing Banishment** — LBRP or similar

### Scrying the Aethyrs

**Method (Crowley's Approach):**
- **Preparation** — Fasting, meditation, ritual purification
- **Invocation** — Call of the 30 Aethyrs (19th Key), naming the specific Aethyr
- **Scrying** — Gaze into crystal, shewstone, or enter trance state
- **Recording** — Speak or write the vision as it unfolds (crucial—do not rely on memory)
- **Integration** — Meditate on the vision's meaning, cross-reference with Qabalistic symbolism

**Warning:** The Aethyrs (especially 10-1) contain overwhelming, ego-dissolving content. Approach with humility, preparation, and respect.

### Safety and Banishing

**Essential Practices:**
- **Never invoke without banishing first and after** — Enochian forces are potent and can destabilize unprepared practitioners
- **Use the LBRP** — The Qabalistic Cross and archangelic invocations provide protective structure
- **Work sequentially** — Do not jump into advanced Aethyrs without traversing the outer ones
- **Keep detailed records** — The material reveals itself over time;記憶 alone is insufficient
- **Discernment** — Not every voice is angelic; test spirits, demand coherence

## Key Enochian Entities

### The Revealing Angels

**Nalvage** — One of the primary angels who revealed the Heptarchia Mystica; described as stern, authoritative, and uncompromising

**Ave** — An angel who appeared frequently to Dee and Kelley, serving as an intermediary and teacher

**Madimi** — A child-like angelic presence who appeared throughout the sessions, often delivering cryptic or playful messages

**Uriel** — One of the four great archangels, who appeared early in the sessions to guide Dee

### The 91 Governors of the Aethyrs

Each Aethyr is governed by **three angels** (except the first, LIL, which has three). Examples:

**30th Aethyr (TEX):** Taoagla, Gemnimb, Advorpt, Dozinal
**10th Aethyr (ZAX):** Lexarph, Comanan, Tabitom (the Guardians of the Abyss)
**1st Aethyr (LIL):** Occodon, Pascomb, Valgars (the highest, dwelling in Kether)

## Fallen Aspect

### Warnings from the Tradition

The Enochian system is **not gentle**. Practitioners report:

- **Rapid destabilization** — Life circumstances shift dramatically, often through crisis
- **Ego confrontation** — The system demands integrity; it exposes self-deception ruthlessly
- **Psychological intensity** — Visions can be terrifying, ecstatic, or incomprehensible
- **Spiritual danger** — Improper invocation can attract malevolent forces or induce psychosis

**Aleister Crowley's Caution:** "The Enochian system is the most powerful ever discovered, but it is also the most dangerous."

### The Abyss (10th Aethyr, ZAX)

The crossing of **Daath** in the Enochian system is marked by the 10th Aethyr. Crowley's vision describes:
- **Choronzon** — The demon of dispersion, who embodies the dissolution of all structure
- **Total annihilation** — The ego is shattered, all certainty destroyed
- **The Guardian** — Only those who have fully integrated their Shadow and surrendered all attachments may pass

Many practitioners stop before the 10th Aethyr. Those who proceed unprepared risk madness.

## Enochian in the Western Esoteric Tradition

### The Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn

The **Golden Dawn** (founded 1888) integrated Enochian heavily into its curriculum:
- **Neophyte Grade:** Basic understanding of the Watchtowers
- **Portal Grade:** Detailed study of elemental attributions
- **Adeptus Minor:** Enochian invocations and Watchtower magic
- **Higher Grades:** Aethyr scrying and advanced Keys

**S.L. MacGregor Mathers** and **William Wynn Westcott** systematized Dee's material, creating the Enochian Chess game, Watchtower ceremonies, and the now-standard color attributions.

### Aleister Crowley and Thelema

**Crowley's Contributions:**
- **The Vision and the Voice** (1911) — Complete scrying record of all 30 Aethyrs
- **Liber Chanokh** — Crowley's commentary on Enochian practice
- **Integration with Thelema** — Mapped Enochian forces to the Thelemic cosmology (e.g., Babalon in the 14th Aethyr)

Crowley considered the Aethyrs to be **the most important magical operation** of his life, surpassing even the reception of *The Book of the Law*.

### Modern Chaos Magic

**Chaos magicians** (Phil Hine, Peter J. Carroll) stripped Enochian of its Qabalistic overlay, using it as:
- **Pure sigil magic** — Watchtower squares as visual glyphs
- **Linguistic sorcery** — Enochian as a "barbarian" language of power
- **Paradigm-shifting** — Treating Enochian as one tool among many, not a singular truth

## Cross-System Integration

### Jungian Psychology

**The Aethyrs** map to stages of **Individuation**:
- **TEX to ZAX (30-10)** — Confrontation with the personal unconscious, Shadow integration
- **ZAX (10)** — The Dark Night of the Soul, encounter with the Self as Void
- **ZID to LIL (9-1)** — Integration of the transpersonal, union with the Divine

**Enochian Scrying** resembles **Active Imagination**—dialoguing with autonomous psychic structures, receiving symbolic communications, integrating unconscious material.

### The Hero's Journey

**The 30 Aethyrs** follow the monomythic structure:
- **TEX-RII (30-23)** — Ordinary World, Call to Adventure, Threshold Crossing
- **BAG-ZAX (22-10)** — Trials, Allies, Enemies, Approach to the Inmost Cave
- **ZAX (10)** — The Ordeal (death and rebirth)
- **ZID-LIL (9-1)** — Reward, Road Back, Resurrection, Return with the Elixir

### Astrology

**Planetary forces** permeate the system:
- **Heptarchia** — Direct planetary magic
- **Watchtower Seniors** — Planetary governance of elemental forces
- **Aethyr Governors** — Some attributed to planetary spheres

**Astrological timing** enhances Enochian work (e.g., invoke Fire Watchtower during Mars hour on Tuesday).

## Liber Loagaeth: The Book of the Speech from God

**Structure:** 49 tables, each containing a 49x49 grid of letters in the Enochian alphabet (total: 120,050 letters)

**Function:** Unknown—Dee and Kelley received the tables but were not given explicit instructions for their use

**Theories:**
- **Meditative focus** — Gazing at the grids induces altered states
- **Angelic language primer** — The tables encode deeper grammatical or mystical structure
- **Cosmic map** — Each table corresponds to a planetary or zodiacal force
- **Unspeakable knowledge** — The content is too advanced for human comprehension

**Modern Use:** Few practitioners work with Liber Loagaeth due to its obscurity. Some use it as a scrying tool or meditative object.

**Hybrid Rituals:** Many magicians open with the LBRP (invoking Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, Uriel) and then proceed to invoke the Enochian Watchtower Kings—layering Qabalistic protection with Enochian potency.

## The Gift of Integration

At its highest expression, the Enochian system offers:

**Direct revelation** — Contact with autonomous intelligences beyond the ego
**Cosmological literacy** — A complete map of the universe from manifestation to source
**Linguistic power** — A sacred language that bypasses the rational mind
**Initiatory structure** — The 30 Aethyrs provide a sequential path of transformation
**Integration of opposites** — Bridging Qabalistic, Hermetic, and Christian mysticism

The system demands **humility, discipline, and relentless self-honesty**. It is not for dabblers or ego-seekers. Those who approach it with reverence and rigor may find it to be **the most comprehensive magical technology ever revealed**.
