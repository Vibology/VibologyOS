# John Dee and Edward Kelley
*The Magus and the Scryer — Renaissance Philosopher and Mystical Medium*

---
## Essence
The [[Enochian Tradition|Enochian system]]—its language, hierarchies, cosmology, and magical technology—was not discovered through scholarship or meditation but **received through direct angelic revelation** to two men: **Dr. John Dee** (1527-1608/9) and **Edward Kelley** (1555-1597). Their partnership, spanning 1581 to 1587, produced one of the most comprehensive and controversial bodies of esoteric knowledge in Western history.

**John Dee** was the architect—mathematician, astronomer, geographer, philosopher, and advisor to Queen Elizabeth I. He **interpreted** the visions, organized the material, and recorded the dialogues.

**Edward Kelley** was the instrument—scryer, clairvoyant, and controversial mystic who **saw and heard** the angels through gazing into polished obsidian or crystal.

Together, they form a paradoxical dyad:
- **Dee:** Rational, devout, seeking divine knowledge to serve God and England
- **Kelley:** Mercurial, questionable character, yet the channel for extraordinary revelations

Their work raises enduring questions: Were the angels real? Was Kelley a charlatan? Did Dee's piety blind him to deception—or did the partnership access something genuinely transpersonal?

## John Dee: The Queen's Magus

### Early Life and Education (1527-1550s)

**Born:** July 13, 1527, in Mortlake, England

**Education:**
- **St. John's College, Cambridge** (B.A. 1545, M.A. 1548)
- **University of Leuven** (Belgium) — Studied with leading European scholars
- Mastered: **Mathematics, astronomy, navigation, geometry, optics, alchemy, Hermetic philosophy**

**Reputation by Age 30:**
- One of the most learned men in England
- Advisor on navigation to explorers (consulted on Northwest Passage expeditions)
- Expert in cartography and geography
- Owned the largest private library in England (over 4,000 volumes)

### Service to the Crown

**Royal Astrologer:**
- Chosen by Queen Elizabeth I to select her coronation date (January 15, 1559) based on astrological auspices
- Consulted on matters of state, espionage, and policy
- Some historians suggest Dee was an intelligence agent (signature "007" in correspondence may have inspired Ian Fleming's James Bond)

**Scientific Contributions:**
- **Mathematics:** Wrote the influential preface to the first English translation of Euclid's *Elements*
- **Navigation:** Improved navigational techniques for English explorers
- **Optics:** Experimented with lenses and mirrors, fueling rumors of "sorcery"

### The Turn to Angelic Communication (1581)

**Crisis of Knowledge:**
By his 50s, Dee had exhausted what human learning could offer. Despite his vast scholarship, he felt **ultimate truth remained hidden**. He turned to **angel magic**—not as superstition, but as an extension of natural philosophy.

**Rationale:**
- **Theological:** Angels possess knowledge of God's creation
- **Philosophical:** Direct revelation could bypass the limitations of human reason
- **Practical:** Dee sought answers to questions no book could answer (the structure of the cosmos, England's divine mission, alchemical secrets)

**First Attempts:**
Dee initially attempted scrying himself but lacked the psychic sensitivity. He employed several scryers before finding Kelley in 1582.

### Dee's Character

**Devout Christian:**
- Never saw his work as heretical—he believed he was serving God
- Prayed extensively before and after angelic sessions
- Interpreted the revelations through a Christian lens (angels as servants of the Biblical God)

**Disciplined Scholar:**
- Meticulously recorded every session in diaries
- Cross-referenced angelic statements with scripture, Qabalah, and Hermetic texts
- Demanded precision—if an angel's statement contradicted itself, Dee would question it

**Naïve or Wise?**
- Critics argue Dee was gullible, exploited by Kelley
- Defenders argue Dee's intellectual rigor prevented total deception—the **internal consistency** of the Enochian system suggests something beyond fraud

## Edward Kelley: The Enigmatic Scryer

### Early Life (1555-1582)

**Born:** August 1, 1555, in Worcester, England (some sources say Ireland)

**Background:**
- Far less documented than Dee
- Some accounts claim he studied law or worked as an apothecary
- Reputation as a dubious character—rumors of forgery, charlatanism, and criminal activity

**The Ear Incident:**
- Kelley's ears were reportedly **cropped** (a punishment for forgery or counterfeiting)
- He often wore a cap to conceal this mutilation
- The incident casts doubt on his reliability, yet it does not disprove his psychic abilities

### Meeting Dee (1582)

**Introduction:**
Kelley (then calling himself "Edward Talbot") was introduced to Dee by a mutual acquaintance in March 1582.

**Immediate Results:**
- Dee tested Kelley's scrying ability
- Kelley reported seeing angelic figures in the shewstone immediately
- The visions were detailed, coherent, and matched Dee's spiritual aspirations

**The Partnership Begins:**
Dee, convinced of Kelley's genuine ability, employed him as his scryer. They would work together for **five years**, traveling across Europe and receiving the bulk of the Enochian revelations.

### Kelley's Abilities

**Scrying Method:**
- Gazed into a **polished obsidian mirror** (Dee's "black stone," now in the British Museum) or a **crystal sphere**
- Reported seeing angelic figures, hearing their voices, and observing symbolic visions
- Dee would question the angels; Kelley would relay their responses

**Characteristics of Kelley's Visions:**
- **Vivid and specific** — Not vague generalities but detailed names, tables, diagrams
- **Consistent** — The Enochian language exhibits grammatical coherence; the cosmology is internally logical
- **Challenging** — The angels often rebuked or tested Dee and Kelley, demanding moral rigor

**Was Kelley Genuine?**

**Arguments for Fraud:**
- His dubious background and cropped ears suggest dishonesty
- He may have invented visions to maintain Dee's patronage
- Some statements from the "angels" seem self-serving (especially the infamous "wife-swapping" incident)

**Arguments for Genuine Ability:**
- The **complexity and coherence** of the material exceed what a 16th-century con artist could improvise
- Kelley often resisted or questioned the angels himself, suggesting he was not simply fabricating what Dee wanted to hear
- The linguistic sophistication of Enochian (grammar, vocabulary, internal consistency) suggests a transpersonal source
- Modern practitioners report that Enochian magic **works**—it produces tangible psychological and synchronistic effects

**Hybrid Theory:**
Kelley may have been **genuinely psychic** but also **psychologically unstable and morally compromised**—a flawed vessel for a real transmission.

## The Angelic Sessions (1581-1587)

### Phase 1: Early Contacts (1581-1582)

**First Scryers:**
Before Kelley, Dee employed **Barnabas Saul** and others, but results were inconsistent.

**First Angels:**
- **Uriel** — One of the four great archangels, appeared early to instruct Dee
- **Nalvage** — Stern and authoritative, delivered the [[Heptarchia Mystica]]
- **Ave** — Gentler, more explanatory, provided the 49 Good Angels

**Revelations:**
- Instructions to construct the **Holy Table** and **Sigillum Dei Aemeth**
- The Heptarchic system of planetary magic
- Divine names and protective seals

### Phase 2: Liber Loagaeth (1583)

**Content:**
49 tables of 49×49 letter grids in the Enochian alphabet—**120,050 letters total**

**Reception:**
Dictated letter-by-letter through Kelley over many sessions

**Purpose:**
The angels called it the "Book of the Speech from God" but provided **no instructions for use**. To this day, its function remains mysterious.

### Phase 3: The Great Table (1584)

**Content:**
[[The Four Watchtowers]]—four elemental tablets containing hierarchies of angels, divine names, and magical squares

**Reception:**
Revealed in stages, with the angels dictating the letters row-by-row and explaining the derivation of names

**Significance:**
The Watchtowers became the **most widely practiced** component of Enochian magic, integrated into the Golden Dawn system and modern ceremonial magic.

### Phase 4: The 30 Aethyrs (1584-1585)

**Content:**
[[The 30 Aethyrs|Thirty concentric spheres of consciousness]], numbered from TEX (30, outermost) to LIL (1, innermost)

**Reception:**
The angels revealed the names of the Aethyrs and their 91 Governors, along with the **48 Enochian Keys** used to invoke them

**Significance:**
The Aethyrs provide a **map of initiation**—a sequential journey from material manifestation to divine unity. Aleister Crowley's scrying of all 30 in 1909 proved their visionary potency.

### Phase 5: European Travels (1583-1587)

**Journey:**
Dee and Kelley, along with their families, traveled across Europe:
- **Poland** (1583-1584) — Patronized by Count Albert Laski
- **Prague** (1584-1586) — Under the court of Emperor Rudolf II
- **Bohemia** (1586-1587)

**Purpose:**
- Seek patronage (Dee's financial situation was precarious)
- Deliver prophetic messages from the angels to European rulers
- Continue the angelic sessions away from English suspicion

**Reception:**
Mixed. Some nobles were intrigued; others accused Dee of sorcery or heresy. Rudolf II eventually expelled them after pressure from the Catholic Church.

### The Wife-Swapping Incident (1587)

**The Revelation:**
In April 1587, Kelley reported that the angels commanded Dee and Kelley to **share their wives** in common—a practice called "cross-matching."

**Dee's Reaction:**
Initially horrified. Dee prayed, resisted, and questioned the angel (identified as "Madimi," often appearing as a child-like figure). After weeks of inner turmoil, Dee reluctantly agreed, believing it was a divine test.

**Aftermath:**
- The arrangement caused **immense strain** on both marriages
- Dee and Kelley's partnership deteriorated
- Modern interpretations vary:
  - **Skeptics:** This proves Kelley was a fraud, manipulating Dee for sexual access
  - **Esotericists:** This was a genuine (if disturbing) test of obedience, reflecting antinomian themes in mystical traditions (breaking taboos to transcend the ego)
  - **Psychologists:** The "angel" may have been a projection of unconscious desires or shadow material

**Separation:**
By 1587, Dee and Kelley parted ways. Dee returned to England. Kelley remained in Bohemia, attempting alchemy and scrying independently.

## Later Lives

### Dee's Return to England (1589-1608)

**Ruin:**
- Dee returned to find his Mortlake library **ransacked and looted**—thousands of books and manuscripts stolen or destroyed
- His reputation suffered—accused of sorcery, shunned by former allies
- Financial hardship—despite royal service, Dee died in poverty

**Final Years:**
- Appointed **Warden of Christ's College, Manchester** (1595-1604)—a minor position far below his stature
- Continued to petition Queen Elizabeth and later King James I for recognition and support—largely ignored
- Died December 1608 or March 1609 in Mortlake, impoverished and largely forgotten

**Legacy:**
Dee's angelic diaries were preserved (now in the British Library and Bodleian Library), ensuring the Enochian system survived.

### Kelley's Tragic End (1587-1597)

**Alchemy and Imprisonment:**
- Kelley remained in Prague, claiming to have achieved the **Philosopher's Stone** (transmutation of base metals into gold)
- Emperor Rudolf II imprisoned Kelley, demanding he produce gold
- Kelley could not deliver, leading to extended imprisonment

**Death:**
- Kelley died in 1597 (or 1595, sources vary) attempting to **escape from prison**
- He either fell from a tower or was killed in the attempt
- Some accounts say he broke his leg in the fall and died of infection

**Ambiguity:**
Kelley's death, like his life, is shrouded in uncertainty. Was he a charlatan who finally faced consequences? A genuine mystic who overreached? A victim of his own ambition?

## The Enochian Legacy

### Preservation of the Material

**Dee's Diaries:**
- *Mysteriorum Libri Quinque* (Five Books of Mystery)
- *Liber Loagaeth*
- Various manuscripts containing the Watchtowers, Aethyrs, and Keys

**Rediscovery:**
- The material languished in obscurity for over 200 years
- In the 19th century, occultists began to study Dee's manuscripts

### The Golden Dawn (1888-1900s)

**S.L. MacGregor Mathers** and **William Wynn Westcott**, founders of the **Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn**, systematized Enochian magic:
- Integrated the Watchtowers into the Golden Dawn curriculum
- Created color correspondences, Enochian Chess, and ritual structures
- Trained members in Enochian invocation as part of advanced grades

**Impact:**
The Golden Dawn made Enochian magic **accessible and teachable**, spreading it to occultists worldwide.

### Aleister Crowley (1909)

**The Vision and the Voice:**
Crowley scried all [[The 30 Aethyrs]] in the Algerian desert, publishing detailed accounts of each vision.

**Contribution:**
- Proved the Aethyrs were **functional**, not just theoretical
- Added Thelemic interpretation (e.g., Babalon in the 14th Aethyr)
- His vision of crossing the Abyss (10th Aethyr, ZAX) remains the definitive description of that ordeal

### Modern Practice

**Chaos Magic:**
- Stripped Enochian of Qabalistic overlay, using it as pure sigil magic and linguistic sorcery

**Ceremonial Magic:**
- Continues to use the full Dee/Kelley system, invoking Watchtowers, Aethyrs, and Heptarchia

**Academic Study:**
- Historians of esotericism study Dee's diaries as primary sources
- Linguistic analysis of Enochian explores its structure and possible origins

## Interpretations: What Really Happened?

### 1. The Spiritualist View

**Claim:** Dee and Kelley genuinely contacted angelic (or at least transpersonal) intelligences.

**Evidence:**
- The complexity and coherence of the material exceed human invention
- The system **works** in practice—practitioners report tangible results
- Dee's character (devout, rigorous) makes deliberate fraud unlikely

### 2. The Skeptical View

**Claim:** Kelley fabricated the visions, exploiting Dee's piety and naivety.

**Evidence:**
- Kelley's dubious background (cropped ears, forgery)
- The "wife-swapping" incident suggests manipulation
- No independent verification of the angels' existence

### 3. The Jungian-Psychological View

**Claim:** The angels were **autonomous psychic structures** (archetypes from the collective unconscious), mediated through Kelley's psyche.

**Evidence:**
- The visions fit archetypal patterns (divine hierarchy, cosmic maps, initiatory trials)
- Similar material arises in other mystical traditions (Qabalah, Gnosticism)
- The partnership created a **liminal space** (Dee's intellect + Kelley's psychic receptivity) where the unconscious could speak

**Implication:** The angels may be **psychologically real** without being **ontologically independent entities**—i.e., real experiences arising from within, not fraudulent, but not "external beings" either.

### 4. The Hybrid View

**Claim:** Kelley was **genuinely psychic** but also **morally flawed**. The material is a **mixture** of:
- Transpersonal revelation (Enochian language, Watchtowers, Aethyrs)
- Kelley's unconscious projections (some of the personal messages, controversial commands)
- Possible deception (wife-swapping may have been Kelley's manipulation, not angelic command)

**Implication:** The Enochian system can be **true** (in the sense of functional and coherent) even if the scryer was imperfect.

## Cross-System Correspondences

### Dee as the Hermetic Magus

Dee embodies the **Hermetic ideal**:
- **Philosopher** (intellectual rigor)
- **Magician** (practical invocation)
- **Seeker of Divine Wisdom** (angelic communication)

**Tarot:** [[The Magician (I)|The Magician]] — Mastery of tools, mediation between divine and earthly
**Jungian:** The **Wise Old Man** archetype—seeking ultimate knowledge

### Kelley as the Mercurial Trickster

Kelley embodies the **Mercurial Trickster**:
- **Shape-shifter** (ambiguous character, cropped ears, name change)
- **Psychopomp** (mediator between worlds, channel for angels)
- **Shadow-bearer** (flawed, morally questionable, yet essential)

**Tarot:** [[The Magician (I)|The Magician]] (reversed) or [[The Fool (0)]]—the chaotic, ungrounded wanderer
**Jungian:** The **Trickster** archetype—necessary chaos, the Shadow that reveals hidden truths

### The Partnership as Syzygy

**Dee + Kelley = Logos + Eros**
- Dee (Logos) = Rational, ordered, structured, solar
- Kelley (Eros) = Intuitive, chaotic, receptive, lunar

Together, they form a **syzygy** (union of opposites), necessary for the work:
- Dee alone could not access the visions
- Kelley alone could not organize or interpret them

## Fallen Aspect

### Dee's Shadow: Naivety and Spiritual Bypassing

**Danger:** Trusting authority (angelic or otherwise) without discernment

**Lesson:** Even devout seekers can be deceived.[^1] Spiritual maturity requires **integration of Shadow**, not just piety.

### Kelley's Shadow: The Exploiter

**Danger:** Using psychic gifts for manipulation and personal gain

**Lesson:** Psychic ability does not equal moral development. Power without integrity leads to ruin.

### The Partnership's Shadow: Co-Dependence

**Danger:** Dee needed Kelley's visions; Kelley needed Dee's patronage—neither could leave, even when the relationship became toxic

**Lesson:** Even sacred work can harbor unhealthy dynamics. Discernment and boundaries are essential.

## The Gift of Integration

Despite ambiguities, the partnership of Dee and Kelley produced:

**A complete magical system** — Functional, coherent, and potent
**A sacred language** — Enochian, the tongue of angels
**A map of consciousness** — The 30 Aethyrs, from manifestation to unity
**A cosmological framework** — Watchtowers, Heptarchia, Aethyrs integrated
**A cautionary tale** — About the perils and promises of seeking ultimate knowledge

Their work endures because it **speaks to something real**—whether that "real" is external angels, archetypal structures, or the transpersonal depths of the human psyche matters less than the **transformative power** the system holds for those who engage it seriously.
