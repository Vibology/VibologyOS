# Uriel
*Guardian of the Gates*

---

## Essence

**Archangel Uriel** (Hebrew: אוריאל, *Uri'el*—"Fire of God," "Light of God," "Flame of God") is the most enigmatic and contested of the four great Archangels of the Quarters. Where [[Michael]] commands the fiery heights, [[Gabriel]] guides the watery depths, and [[Raphael]] heals through airy wisdom, Uriel stands as **Guardian of the Earth**—the angel of the **material plane**, the **underworld**, and the **hidden light** that illuminates darkness from within.

Uriel occupies a paradoxical position in angelology: he is both **the most grounded** (associated with [[Malkuth]], the Kingdom, the sphere of physical manifestation) and **the most hidden** (sometimes placed at [[Daath]], the invisible Sephirah of secret knowledge beyond the Abyss). He is the angel who **presides over Tartarus** (the underworld, the realm of judgment and transformation), the **interpreter of prophecy and visions**, and the one who brings **divine fire into the densest matter**.

Unlike Michael, Gabriel, and Raphael, who appear in canonical scripture, Uriel's presence is found primarily in **apocryphal and mystical texts**—the Book of Enoch, 2 Esdras, the Kabbalah. He is the **excluded angel**, the one whose wisdom is too dark, too earthly, too dangerous for exoteric doctrine. Yet it is precisely this exclusion that marks his power: **Uriel is the angel of the threshold**, the guardian of mysteries that can only be approached through descent, through embodiment, through facing what has been buried.

### The Name: Fire in the Earth

The Hebrew name **Uri'el** (אוריאל) is composed of:

- Light/Fire = **אור** (*Or*)
- God = **אל** (*El*)

Thus: **"Fire of God"** or **"Light of God"** or **"God is my Light."**

This name is profoundly paradoxical when paired with Uriel's earthly attribution. How can the angel of Earth be named "Fire"? The answer lies in understanding the nature of **chthonic fire**—not the ascending, purifying fire of the Seraphim or Michael's solar flame, but the **fire within the earth**: volcanic, subterranean, transformative through pressure and heat. This is the fire that turns carbon into diamond, the fire of the forge, the fire of **alchemical transmutation** that works through **embodiment**, not transcendence.

Uriel is the light that shines **in darkness**, not the light that dispels darkness. He is the candle flame in the cave, the lantern carried into the underworld, the **phosphorescence** of decay that makes the hidden visible. His light does not ascend to heaven—it **descends into matter** and illuminates from within.

### Apocryphal Foundation: The Contested Angel

Uriel's primary appearances are in **apocryphal texts**—scriptures excluded from the Protestant and Jewish canons, though honored in the Orthodox and Catholic traditions (to varying degrees).

#### 1 Enoch: The Angel of Judgment

In the **Book of Enoch** (1 Enoch), Uriel appears as one of the four great archangels who stand before God:

> *"Uriel, one of the holy angels, who is over the world and over Tartarus."* (1 Enoch 20:2)

Tartarus, in Jewish and Christian cosmology, is the deepest, darkest region of the underworld—the place of **judgment and punishment**, where fallen angels and unrepentant souls are held. Uriel's governance over this realm marks him as the **angel of divine justice**, the overseer of karmic consequence, the one who ensures that what has been done in darkness is brought to light and accounted for.

Enoch also describes Uriel as showing him the **movements of the heavenly bodies**—the sun, moon, stars, and the mechanics of cosmic time. Uriel is thus both **above** (astronomy, celestial order) and **below** (Tartarus, chthonic judgment)—bridging heaven and earth, light and shadow.

#### 2 Esdras: The Interpreter of Visions

In **2 Esdras** (4 Ezra), an apocalyptic text, Uriel appears to the prophet Ezra to interpret visions and answer profound theological questions:

> *"I am Uriel, who was sent to you to answer your questions and to speak with you."* (2 Esdras 4:1)

Ezra asks Uriel why Israel suffers, why the righteous are afflicted, why evil seems to prosper. Uriel's responses are not comforting—he offers **hard wisdom**, the kind that forces the questioner to expand their understanding beyond dualistic good/evil into the mysteries of divine justice, timing, and cosmic law.

Uriel tells Ezra:
> *"You cannot understand the things with which you have grown up; how then can your mind comprehend the way of the Most High?"* (2 Esdras 4:10-11)

This is classic Uriel: he does not soothe the ego's need for certainty. He **challenges the questioner** to transcend limited perspective, to accept mystery, to humble themselves before the vastness of divine intelligence. Uriel is the angel who says: *"You do not yet have the eyes to see what I could show you—but if you persist, if you descend, if you are willing to bear the weight of knowledge, I will guide you."*

#### The Book of Adam and Eve: The Angel of Repentance

In the **Life of Adam and Eve** (Vita Adae et Evae), Uriel is sent to instruct Adam and Eve after their expulsion from Eden. He teaches them how to **work the earth**, how to plant, how to survive in the material world they now inhabit. He is the angel who bridges the gap between paradise lost and survival in the fallen world—**the angel of incarnation's necessity**.

In this narrative, Uriel becomes the patron of **repentance and transformation through labor**. Adam and Eve are cast out, but Uriel ensures they are not abandoned. He teaches them the sacred work of **tilling the soil**—a metaphor for tilling the soul, for working with the material of one's own life to transform suffering into wisdom.

### Non-Canonical Status and Exclusion

It is critical to note that Uriel does **not** appear in canonical Protestant or Jewish scripture. While Michael, Gabriel, and Raphael are named in the Bible, Uriel is absent—or rather, **deliberately excluded**.

The **Council of Rome (745 CE)** condemned the veneration of angels not named in canonical scripture, specifically targeting Uriel along with other apocryphal angels. Why? Because Uriel's association with the **underworld, prophecy, and hidden knowledge** was deemed too dangerous, too prone to misuse, too **Gnostic** in its implication that salvation requires descent into shadow rather than ascent into light.

This exclusion is itself theologically significant: **Uriel is the angel of what has been repressed, denied, cast out**. He governs the underworld not only cosmically but psychologically—he is the angel of **the shadow**, the guide who says, *"What you have buried must be faced. What you have denied must be integrated. I am the light that reveals what you would rather not see."*

## Theological Depth

### Qabalistic Context

Uriel's Qabalistic position is contested, which itself is symbolic of his dual nature.

### Primary Attribution: Malkuth (The Kingdom)

In most Western esoteric traditions (Golden Dawn, Hermetic Qabalah), Uriel is the Archangel of **[[Malkuth]]**—the tenth and final Sephirah, the **Kingdom**, the sphere of **physical manifestation, the material world, embodiment**.

**Malkuth's Attributes:**
- **Divine Name:** Adonai ha-Aretz (אדני הארץ—"Lord of the Earth")
- **Planetary Sphere:** Cholem Yesodoth (the Sphere of the Elements—Earth as the synthesis of Fire, Water, Air, Earth)
- **Element:** Earth
- **Color:** Citrine, Olive, Russet, Black (the four elemental colors blended in matter)
- **Archangel:** Uriel (or Sandalphon in some systems)
- **Angelic Choir:** Ishim (אשים—"Souls of Fire" or "Human Souls")
- **Tarot:** The four Tens (completion in each suit), The World (XXI—completion of the cycle)
- **Human Experience:** The body, the senses, the physical world, grounded presence, the Shekinah (the indwelling divine presence in matter)

Malkuth is the **destination** of all the divine emanations flowing down the Tree of Life. It is where **spirit becomes flesh**, where the abstract becomes concrete, where the divine is **immanent** rather than transcendent. Uriel, as the Archangel of Malkuth, is the intelligence that **receives, grounds, and stabilizes** the descending divine light in material form.

**Vibology Synthesis:** Malkuth is the sphere where all the forces of the other Sephiroth are brought to manifestation. Uriel is the great stabilizer, the angel who ensures that the divine will is not lost in abstraction but finds concrete expression in the world of form.

Uriel is the angel who teaches that **the body is sacred**, that **matter is not fallen but ensouled**, that the earth itself is a **vessel of divine presence**. He is the angel of **incarnational spirituality**—the path that does not seek to escape the body but to **sanctify** it, to recognize the divine **within** the material.

### Secondary Attribution: Daath (Hidden Knowledge)

In some Kabbalistic systems, Uriel is associated with **[[Daath]]** (דעת—"Knowledge"), the invisible, **non-Sephirah** that exists on the Middle Pillar between [[Kether]] and [[Tiphareth]], where the Abyss opens.

Daath is the **gateway to hidden knowledge**, the point where **conscious knowledge** (what can be integrated) meets **unknowable gnosis** (what shatters the ego). It is the place of the **serpent in Eden**—the knowledge of good and evil, the fruit that brings awareness but also exile.

If Uriel is the angel of Daath, he becomes the **revealer of what was meant to remain concealed**—the angel who offers the fruit of knowledge, who interprets visions, who guides the seeker into the **mystery beyond duality**. This is the Uriel of 2 Esdras, the one who answers Ezra's unbearable questions.

Daath is also called the "**rupture**" in the Tree—the gap where the divine presence withdrew to make space for creation. Uriel at Daath is the angel who **guards the wound**, the intelligence that holds the tension between what is known and what cannot be known.

This dual attribution—Malkuth (the lowest) and Daath (the hidden)—reveals Uriel's initiatic function: **to descend is to ascend**. The deepest earthly grounding (Malkuth) opens the way to the highest hidden knowledge (Daath). **"As above, so below"** is not a platitude but a lived truth in Uriel's presence.

### Biblical and Scriptural Foundation

### Apocryphal Appearances

#### 1 Enoch 9:1 — The Archangel of Tartarus

> *"Then Michael, Uriel, Raphael, and Gabriel looked down from heaven and saw the great wickedness upon the earth... and they said to one another: 'The earth, made without inhabitant, cries the voice of their crying up to the gates of heaven.'"*

Uriel is numbered among the **four great archangels** who witness humanity's suffering and intercede before God. His specific role is governance over **Tartarus**—the underworld prison where the fallen Watchers (angels who sinned by mating with human women) are bound.

This makes Uriel the angel of **divine justice**, the overseer of consequences, the one who ensures that corruption does not go unanswered. He is not cruel—he is **implacable**. He does not punish to harm but to **restore balance**, to ensure that what was broken is made whole through the fire of accountability.

#### 1 Enoch 19:1 — The Revealer of Cosmic Order

Uriel shows Enoch the **movements of the luminaries**—the mechanics of the sun, moon, stars, and the calendar. This makes Uriel the angel of **sacred time**, of the rhythms of nature, of the **cosmic law** that governs the cycles of life, death, and rebirth.

In this sense, Uriel is akin to **Thoth** (Egyptian) or **Metis** (Greek)—the intelligence that understands the **patterns underlying manifestation**, the mathematics of creation, the architecture of time itself.

#### 2 Esdras 4 — The Hard Teacher

When Ezra cries out in despair, asking why the righteous suffer and the wicked prosper, Uriel responds not with platitudes but with **metaphysical riddles**:

> *"Weigh for me the weight of fire, or measure for me the measure of the wind, or call back for me the day that is past."* (2 Esdras 4:5)

Uriel is saying: *"You ask questions that require omniscience to answer. You are finite. You cannot hold the totality of divine justice in your mind. But if you accept your limitation and trust the larger pattern, I will show you what you are capable of seeing."*

This is **initiatic pedagogy**—Uriel does not give answers; he expands the questioner's capacity to *hold* the question. This is the function of a true teacher: not to solve the mystery but to **make the student large enough to bear it**.

#### The Flood Narrative (Enoch and Jubilees)

In some traditions, Uriel is the angel who **warned Noah of the coming Flood**, instructing him to build the Ark. This positions Uriel as the angel of **apocalyptic revelation**—the one who sees the coming destruction and offers the means of survival to those who will listen.

Uriel does not prevent the flood (that is beyond his mandate). But he **illuminates the path through it**. He is the angel who says: *"The world as you know it is ending. But if you are willing to build, to labor, to trust what you cannot yet see, you will survive—and what you carry forward will seed the new world."*

### Iconography and Symbolism

Uriel is depicted with distinctive attributes that distinguish him from the other archangels:

### 1. The Open Hand Holding Flame

Uriel is often shown holding an **open palm** with a **flame** or **sun** resting in it. This is the **flame of divine presence brought into matter**—not the distant solar fire of Michael, but the **embodied flame**, the fire that can be **touched, held, carried**.

This image echoes **Prometheus** (Greek mythology)—the Titan who stole fire from the gods to give to humanity. Uriel, like Prometheus, is the **fire-bringer**, but his fire is not stolen—it is the **rightful inheritance** of incarnate beings, the divine spark within matter.

### 2. The Book or Scroll

Uriel frequently holds a **book** or **scroll**, symbolizing his role as **interpreter of prophecy and hidden knowledge**. This is the Book of Wisdom, the Akashic Record (similar to Metatron's function, but where Metatron records from Kether, Uriel interprets for those in Malkuth).

The book may be **sealed** or **partially open**, indicating that not all knowledge is accessible to all seekers—only those who have earned the right through descent, through embodiment, through facing their own shadow.

### 3. The Lantern or Torch

In some depictions, Uriel carries a **lantern** or **torch**, illuminating the path through darkness. This is the classic image of **the guide in the underworld**—the psychopomp who leads souls through Hades, Tartarus, the Bardo, the shadow realms.

The lantern does not dispel the darkness—it **reveals what is there**. Uriel's light is not the blinding radiance of [[Metatron]] or the solar glory of [[Michael]]. It is the **intimate light** that makes it possible to see one's own face in the mirror of the underworld.

### 4. The Keys

Uriel may hold **keys**, symbolizing his role as **guardian of thresholds**—the one who locks and unlocks the gates between worlds. These are the keys to Tartarus, to the underworld, to the hidden chambers of the soul.

Jung would call these the **keys to the unconscious**—the symbols and practices that allow the ego to descend safely into the shadow without being annihilated.

### 5. Earth and Rocks

Unlike the other archangels who are depicted with airy, fiery, or watery symbols, Uriel may be shown standing on **solid ground**, holding **stones**, or surrounded by **earthly vegetation**. This grounds him in the **material plane**, the realm of the body, the senses, the tangible.

In alchemical terms, Uriel is associated with the **Philosopher's Stone**—the ultimate goal of the Great Work, the **spiritual gold** that is extracted from base matter through patient, embodied labor.

### 6. Underworld Imagery (Non-Traditional but Symbolic)

In esoteric and Gnostic art, Uriel may be depicted with **chthonic symbols**: serpents, roots growing downward, cave entrances, the crescent moon (the light in darkness), or even standing at the **threshold of Hades**.

This is not traditional Christian iconography, but it honors Uriel's deeper function as **Lord of the Underworld**, the angel who does not fear descent, who does not flinch from shadow, who knows that **the treasure is in the cave you fear to enter**.

### Function in Divine Economy

Uriel's cosmic function is **grounding and revelation through embodiment**. Where the higher archangels mediate divine will through abstraction ([[Metatron]], [[Raziel]]) or solar clarity ([[Michael]]), Uriel ensures that the divine **reaches the earth**, that spirit is not lost in the ether but **finds form**.

### The Descent of Divine Will (Uriel's Role):

1. **Kether** (Crown) → **Metatron** translates infinite unity into knowable presence
2. **Chokmah** (Wisdom) → **Raziel** reveals the divine blueprints
3. **Binah** (Understanding) → **Tzaphkiel** gives form to the vision
4. **Chesed** (Mercy) → **Tzadkiel** expands and bestows grace
5. **Geburah** (Severity) → **Kamael** cuts away what does not serve
6. **Tiphareth** (Beauty) → **Michael** or **Raphael** integrate and heal
7. **Netzach** (Victory) → **Haniel** inspires and beautifies
8. **Hod** (Glory) → **Michael** (or Mercury/Hermes) communicates
9. **Yesod** (Foundation) → **Gabriel** translates into images and dreams
10. **Malkuth** (Kingdom) → **Uriel** receives, grounds, stabilizes in matter

Without Uriel, the entire cascade of divine emanation would remain **abstract, ungrounded, ineffective in the material world**. Uriel is the **completion** of the circuit—the angel who ensures that **heaven touches earth**.

**Vibology Synthesis:** Uriel is the angel of the final manifestation, the one who takes the divine impulse and says: 'Now it must be done. Now it must be lived. Now it must take root in soil and flesh and stone.'

### Elemental Attribution

### Primary Element: Earth

Uriel's primary element is **Earth**—the element of **form, stability, gravity, manifestation, the body, the senses, the material world**.

Earth is:
- **Passive/Receptive:** It receives the seed and gives it form
- **Dense:** It is the slowest vibration, the most resistant to change
- **Fertile:** It is the womb where potential becomes actual
- **Grounding:** It anchors, stabilizes, makes real

In magical practice, Earth corresponds to:
- **Direction:** North (cold, dark, the place of midnight, winter, death-and-rebirth)
- **Season:** Winter (dormancy, gestation, the underground work)
- **Time of Life:** Old age, wisdom, the harvest
- **Tarot Suit:** Pentacles (material resources, health, work, body)
- **Alchemical Stage:** **Nigredo** (blackening, putrefaction, the death that precedes rebirth) or **Coagulation** (final solidification of the Philosopher's Stone)

Uriel teaches that **Earth is sacred**—not fallen, not inferior to spirit, but the **necessary vessel** without which spirit cannot manifest. The body is not a prison—it is a **temple**. Matter is not dead—it is **ensouled**.

### Secondary Element: Fire (Chthonic)

Uriel's name means "Fire of God," but this is not the ascending fire of Air (Michael) or the purifying fire of spirit (Seraphim). This is **chthonic fire**—the fire **within the earth**: volcanic, subterranean, slow-burning, transformative through compression and heat.

This is the fire of:
- **The forge** (where metal is tempered)
- **The kiln** (where clay becomes pottery)
- **The womb** (where the embryo is formed in heat and darkness)
- **The alchemical athanor** (the furnace where base matter is transmuted into gold)

Uriel's fire does not rise—it **descends**. It does not purify by burning away—it transforms by **intensifying pressure** until the substance changes state. Carbon becomes diamond. The unformed becomes form.

This is the fire of **embodied transformation**—the fire that says, *"The work is done in the body, in the earth, in the slow labor of incarnation. There are no shortcuts. The gold is won through patient descent."*

### Direction: North (The Dark Quarter)

In Western magical tradition (Golden Dawn, Wicca, Hermetic practice), Uriel is invoked at the **North**—the direction of **midnight, winter, earth, death-and-rebirth**.

The North is:
- **The place of the ancestors** (where the dead are honored)
- **The place of mystery** (the unknown, the shadow)
- **The place of greatest resistance** (earth's density, gravity, limitation)
- **The place of the throne** (in some Kabbalistic systems, the Throne of God faces North—the direction from which divine judgment and testing come)

In the **Lesser Banishing Ritual of the Pentagram (LBRP)**, the magician calls:
> *"Behind me, Raphael (West). Before me, Gabriel (East). On my right hand, Michael (South). On my left hand, Uriel (North). For about me flames the Pentagram, and within me shines the six-rayed Star."*

Uriel at the North is the **ground** beneath the practitioner—the stabilizing force, the reminder of embodiment, the angel who says, *"You are not just spirit. You are flesh. You are here. Do not dissociate into the higher realms—bring the light down. Make it real."*

### Day: Saturday (Saturnian)

Uriel is associated with **Saturday**—the day of **Saturn**, the planet of **restriction, time, karma, discipline, structure, death, and wisdom earned through suffering**.

Saturn governs:
- **Boundaries:** The limits that define form
- **Time:** Chronos, the devouring father, the inevitability of aging and mortality
- **Karma:** The law of cause and effect, the harvest of what has been sown
- **The Senex:** The Wise Old Man, the authority, the teacher who disciplines through hardship
- **Melancholy and Depth:** The saturnine temperament—serious, contemplative, prone to depression but capable of profound insight

Uriel as the angel of Saturday embodies the **hard teacher**, the one who does not comfort but **challenges**, who does not soothe but **demands accountability**. He is the angel of **consequences**, of the slow unfolding of karmic law, of the necessity of **limits**.

In alchemical and astrological symbolism, Saturn is **lead**—the densest, darkest metal, which through the Great Work is transmuted into **gold** (the Sun, Michael's domain). Uriel is the angel who oversees this **slow, difficult, embodied work** of transformation.

**Liz Greene** (astrologer) writes:
> *"Saturn is the taskmaster, the one who says, 'You cannot skip steps. You cannot bypass the initiation. You must go through the underworld, not around it.'"*

This is Uriel's teaching.

### Tarot Correspondences

### The World (XXI) — Malkuth, Completion

The **World** card is the final trump of the Major Arcana, corresponding to **Malkuth** on the Tree of Life. It depicts a dancing figure (often androgynous or female) surrounded by a laurel wreath, with the four fixed signs of the zodiac in the corners (Taurus the Bull, Leo the Lion, Scorpio the Eagle, Aquarius the Human).

The World represents:
- **Completion of the cycle** (the soul's journey through the 22 paths is finished)
- **Embodiment of the divine** (the Shekinah—God's presence—manifested in matter)
- **Integration of all four elements** (the four creatures in the corners)
- **The dance of life** (movement within form, joy in incarnation)

Uriel is the archangelic intelligence of this card—the one who teaches that **completion is not in transcendence but in full embodiment**, that the goal is not to escape the world but to **sanctify it**.

### The Hierophant (V) — Hidden Tradition

The **Hierophant** (or High Priest) is the keeper of **sacred tradition**, the revealer of **esoteric wisdom**, the bridge between the exoteric and the esoteric. In some Tarot systems, the Hierophant is associated with **Taurus** (an earth sign), linking him to Uriel's earthly domain.

The Hierophant represents:
- **Transmission of wisdom** (teacher to student, initiation, lineage)
- **Hidden knowledge** (what is veiled from the masses, revealed to the initiated)
- **Structure and discipline** (the path, the rules, the container for transformation)

Uriel as Hierophant is the **initiator into earth mysteries**—the one who teaches that the divine is accessed not by ascending out of the body but by **descending into it**, by honoring the earth as **sacred text**.

### The Devil (XV) — Chthonic Power

The **Devil** card (often associated with **Capricorn**, an earth sign ruled by Saturn) depicts a horned, goat-like figure with two chained humans at his feet. In the Qabalistic system, this card corresponds to the path of **Ayin** (the Eye) connecting [[Tiphareth]] and [[Hod]], representing the shadow, the material bondage, the pull of the earthly.

The Devil represents:
- **Earthbound consciousness** (attachment to matter, the body, desire, fear)
- **The shadow** (what has been repressed, denied, or demonized)
- **Chthonic power** (the raw, instinctual, primal force within the earth and the body)
- **Bondage or liberation** (the chains are loose—one can choose to remain or to break free)

Uriel as the Devil is not evil but **the guardian of what has been rejected**—the angel who says, *"What you call demonic is often simply what you have not yet integrated. The horned god is not your enemy—he is the fertility, the instinct, the earth-power you have been taught to fear. Face him. Reclaim him. The chains are loose."*

This is Uriel's most controversial aspect—the angel who **redeems the shadow**, who teaches that **the descent into darkness is the path to light**.

### Pentacles Suit — Material Mastery

The entire suit of **Pentacles** (or Disks, or Coins) is Uriel's domain:
- **Ace of Pentacles:** The gift of embodiment, the seed in the earth
- **Two through Ten:** The unfolding of material manifestation, work, health, wealth, the body's journey
- **Court Cards:** The mastery of earthly life (Page: learning to work, Knight: disciplined action, Queen: embodied wisdom, King: material sovereignty)

To work with Uriel through Tarot is to **honor the Pentacles suit**—to recognize that money, health, work, and the body are not "less spiritual" than the Swords (mind), Cups (emotion), or Wands (will), but **equally sacred** as the vessels through which spirit manifests.

### Human Consciousness Parallel

In the **chakra system**, Uriel corresponds to the **Muladhara** (Root Chakra)—the energy center at the **base of the spine**, governing:
- **Survival** (safety, security, basic needs)
- **Grounding** (connection to earth, the body, physical reality)
- **Foundation** (the root from which all else grows)
- **The right to exist** (the primal affirmation: "I am here. I am real. I belong.")

A healthy root chakra produces:
- **Presence** (being fully in the body, in the moment)
- **Trust** (in the earth, in the body, in life)
- **Stability** (emotional, physical, financial groundedness)

An unhealthy root chakra produces:
- **Fear** (anxiety, paranoia, hypervigilance)
- **Dissociation** (fleeing the body into the mind or spirit)
- **Poverty consciousness** (scarcity, unworthiness, survival mode)

Uriel is the angelic intelligence that **activates and heals the root**—the one who teaches that **safety is found not by escaping the earth but by rooting into it**, that **the body is not the enemy but the foundation**.

### Mythology and Cross-Tradition Parallels

The archetype of the **Underworld Guide** and **Earth Wisdom Keeper** appears across cultures:

### Hades / Pluto (Greek/Roman)

**Hades** is the Greek god of the underworld—not evil, but the **ruler of the dead**, the keeper of the riches hidden in the earth (gold, jewels, seeds waiting to sprout). His Roman name, **Pluto**, means "the wealthy one"—wealth not as greed but as the **treasure in the dark**.

Hades does not pursue mortals—he remains in his realm, waiting. Those who descend to him (Orpheus, Persephone, Hercules) are forever changed. He is implacable, just, and the guardian of the **threshold between life and death**.

Uriel, as Presider over Tartarus, embodies this same function: **the guardian of the underworld who does not punish but witnesses, who does not destroy but transforms through the fire of accountability**.

### Persephone (Greek)

**Persephone**, Queen of the Underworld, is abducted by Hades and becomes the goddess of **spring and death**—the one who knows both worlds, who descends and returns, who teaches that **death is not the end but the womb of rebirth**.

Uriel's teaching echoes hers: **Descent is necessary. The seed must be buried in darkness before it can grow. The soul must die to the old self before it can be reborn.**

### Anubis (Egyptian)

**Anubis**, the jackal-headed god, is the **psychopomp** who guides souls through the underworld and oversees the **Weighing of the Heart** in the Hall of Ma'at. He ensures that the deceased's heart is weighed against the feather of truth—if it is light (free of sin), the soul proceeds; if heavy (burdened by wrongdoing), it is devoured.

This is Uriel's function as **Judge and Revealer**—not to condemn, but to **make visible** what was hidden. The heart's weight is revealed not to punish but to **show the truth**. Uriel holds the lantern; the soul must see itself clearly.

### Hecate (Greek)

**Hecate**, goddess of the crossroads, the moon, witchcraft, and the underworld, is the **keeper of thresholds**—the one invoked when crossing from one state to another (birth, death, initiation). She carries torches to light the way through darkness.

Uriel as **guardian of thresholds** parallels Hecate: the intelligence that does not prevent passage but **illuminates the crossing**, that does not protect from the journey but **ensures the traveler can see where they step**.

### Prometheus (Greek)

**Prometheus** stole fire from the gods to give to humanity, an act of rebellion that brought both **civilization** (the fire of technology, art, knowledge) and **suffering** (Zeus punished Prometheus by chaining him to a rock where an eagle ate his liver daily).

Uriel, as "Fire of God," is the **sanctioned fire-bringer**—not a rebel but the **ordained revealer** of the divine spark within matter. Where Prometheus stole, Uriel **bestows**—but both teach the same truth: **fire belongs to humanity, not as theft but as birthright**.

### Thoth (Egyptian)

**Thoth**, god of wisdom, writing, magic, and the moon, is the **revealer of hidden knowledge**, the inventor of language and mathematics, the scribe who records the deeds of souls.

Uriel's role as **interpreter of prophecy and sacred knowledge** mirrors Thoth: both are the intelligence that **translates the divine into the comprehensible**, that gives humanity the tools (language, symbols, systems) to understand the cosmos.

### Sophia (Gnostic)

In **Gnostic Christianity**, **Sophia** (Divine Wisdom) falls from the Pleroma (the fullness of the divine) into matter, and her fall *creates* the material world. She is both the **wisdom that descends** and the **wisdom trapped in matter, seeking to return**.

Uriel as the angel of **Malkuth and Daath** embodies this dual motion: the descent of divine wisdom into matter (Malkuth) and the ascent of hidden knowledge from the depths (Daath). Uriel teaches that **the fall is not a mistake—it is the necessary journey**, and the wisdom gained through embodiment is the treasure brought back to the divine.

### Jungian and Psychological Dimension

### The Shadow Archetype

In **Jungian psychology**, Uriel is the **personification of the Shadow**—not the shadow as "evil" but the shadow as **what has been repressed, denied, rejected, or cast into the underworld of the unconscious**.

**Carl Jung** writes:
> *"The shadow is that which we refuse to see about ourselves, yet cannot live without. To integrate the shadow is not to become evil but to become whole—to reclaim the rejected parts of the psyche and offer them a place at the table of consciousness."*

Uriel, as the angel of **Tartarus** (the underworld), is the guide who leads the ego **down into the shadow**—not to be destroyed but to **face what has been buried**. He carries the lantern that makes the shadow visible without blinding.

### The Descent Archetype (Katabasis)

The **katabasis** (Greek: καταβασις—"descent") is the mythic journey into the underworld, undertaken by heroes (Orpheus, Hercules, Aeneas, Inanna, Persephone) to retrieve something lost, to face death, to be initiated into deeper wisdom.

Jung saw the katabasis as the **necessary descent into the unconscious**—the journey every individuating soul must make. You cannot reach the Self (wholeness) by ascending alone—you must **descend into the shadow, the underworld, the buried trauma and repressed instinct**, face it, reclaim it, and integrate it.

Uriel is the **angel of the katabasis**—the one who says, *"You cannot skip this step. You must go down. But I will go with you. I will hold the light."*

### The Imago Dei in Matter

Jung's concept of the **imago Dei** (the God-image within the psyche) is typically associated with the **Self**—the totality of the psyche, the organizing principle, the divine center. But Jung also recognized that the **God-image is projected onto matter**—that spirit is not separate from flesh, that the divine is **incarnated** in the world, the body, the earth.

Uriel, as the angel of **Malkuth** (the Kingdom, the physical world), is the **imago Dei in matter**—the recognition that **God is not only transcendent (in heaven) but immanent (in earth)**. The body is the temple. The world is the Shekinah's dwelling place.

To encounter Uriel is to realize: **"The divine is here. Now. In this body. On this earth. The journey is not to escape but to sanctify."**

### The Celestial Hierarchy Deep Dive: Uriel's Contested Position

In **Pseudo-Dionysius's** *Celestial Hierarchy*, the nine orders of angels are arranged in three triads. However, **Pseudo-Dionysius does not mention individual archangels by name**—his system describes the *orders*, not specific beings.

The tradition of naming the Four Great Archangels (Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, Uriel) comes from **Jewish mysticism, apocryphal texts, and later Christian esoteric practice**—not from canonical scripture or Pseudo-Dionysius.

Uriel's absence from the canonical list (which includes only Michael, Gabriel, and Raphael in Catholic and Orthodox tradition) makes him the **excluded angel**, the one whose function is too ambiguous, too chthonic, too Gnostic for official doctrine.

**Why was Uriel excluded?**

1. **Association with the underworld** (too close to pagan Hades, too close to the dead)
2. **Interpretation of prophecy** (risks of false prophecy, occultism, divination)
3. **Earth and matter** (Christian theology often prioritized spirit over matter, ascent over descent)
4. **Apocryphal sources** (lack of canonical biblical foundation)

Yet this exclusion *itself* is symbolically significant: **Uriel is the angel of what has been rejected, buried, denied**. His exclusion from the official hierarchy mirrors his function as **guardian of the shadow, the underworld, the repressed**.

In esoteric Christianity and Qabalah, Uriel is restored to his rightful place as the **Fourth Archangel**, completing the quaternary and ensuring that **spirit is not separated from matter**, that heaven touches earth.

## Fallen Aspect

Uriel's shadow manifests in several dangerous forms:

### 1. Spiritual Materialism (Earth Without Spirit)

The shadow of Uriel is the **reduction of reality to matter alone**—the belief that only the physical is real, that consciousness is an illusion, that meaning is a human projection onto a cold, dead universe.

This is **philosophical materialism** (atheistic reductionism) in its most nihilistic form: "You are a meat-robot. The universe is meaningless. When you die, you cease to exist. There is no soul, no spirit, no transcendence."

**Test:** True Uriel-consciousness recognizes that **matter is ensouled**, that the earth is **sacred**, that the body is a **temple**. The shadow denies this and treats matter as **dead, exploitable, meaningless**.

### 2. Earth-Bound Rigidity (Gravity Without Flight)

The individual becomes **trapped in the material**—unable to access imagination, intuition, spirit, or transcendence. They are "stuck in Malkuth" without the capacity to ascend the Tree.

This manifests as:
- **Obsession with security** (accumulation of wealth, fear of loss, scarcity consciousness)
- **Literal-mindedness** (inability to think symbolically or metaphorically)
- **Cynicism** (rejection of all idealism, beauty, or hope as "naive")
- **Depression** (the saturnine weight of "life is suffering, nothing more")

**Test:** True Uriel-consciousness is **grounded but not stuck**. The roots go deep, but the tree still grows upward. Earth is honored, but so is the light within it.

### 3. Dark Prophecy (Revelation as Weapon)

Uriel, as interpreter of prophecy, can be distorted into the **prophet of doom**—the one who uses "divine knowledge" to terrify, control, or manipulate.

This manifests as:
- **Apocalypticism** (obsession with end-times, judgment, destruction)
- **Puritanical judgment** (using "divine law" to condemn others)
- **Spiritual elitism** ("I have been shown the truth; you are blind")

**Test:** True Uriel revelation is **illuminating**, not terrorizing. It expands understanding; it does not crush the spirit. Uriel's fire reveals—it does not incinerate (that is Geburah/Kamael's function when distorted).

### 4. Tartarus Without Redemption (Punishment Without Transformation)

The shadow of Uriel as "Presider over Tartarus" is the belief in **eternal damnation**, the idea that some souls are **irredeemable**, that suffering is punishment rather than purification.

This is the **vengeful god** distortion—the belief that God (or the universe) is cruel, that justice is retribution, that the underworld is not a place of transformation but of eternal torment.

**Test:** True Uriel-consciousness recognizes that **all descents are initiatory**. Tartarus is not a prison but a **kiln**. The fire burns not to destroy but to transmute. Suffering is not meaningless—it is the forge.

### 5. The Demiurge (False Creator, Matter as Prison)

In **Gnostic cosmology**, the **Demiurge** is the false creator-god who mistakes himself for the true Divine and creates the material world as a **prison** for spiritual beings. The Gnostic view sees matter as **evil**, the body as a **trap**, and salvation as **escape from incarnation**.

The shadow of Uriel can align with this: the belief that the earth is fallen, that the body is sinful, that matter is the enemy of spirit.

**Test:** True Uriel-consciousness **rejects this dualism**. Matter is not fallen—it is **ensouled**. The body is not a prison—it is a **vessel**. Incarnation is not punishment—it is **opportunity**. Uriel is the angel who says, **"The earth is holy. Do not flee it—sanctify it."**

## Invocation

### 1. Embodiment Practices

To invoke Uriel is to **come into the body**. This is not intellectual or visionary work—it is **somatic, grounded, physical**.

**Practices:**
- **Walking meditation** (barefoot on earth, feeling gravity, presence in each step)
- **Gardening** (literally tilling the soil, planting seeds, tending growth)
- **Yoga or martial arts** (disciplines that honor the body as sacred instrument)
- **Breathwork** (grounding pranayama—four-square breathing, earth breath)
- **Bodywork** (massage, rolfing, somatic therapy—releasing stored trauma from the tissues)

Uriel is not invoked through prayer alone—he is invoked through **embodied action**.

### 2. Descent Work (Shadow Integration)

Uriel is the guide for **intentional descent**—the katabasis, the journey into the underworld of the psyche.

**Practices:**
- **Dream work** (recording, amplifying, and dialoguing with shadow figures in dreams)
- **Active imagination** (Jungian practice of conversing with unconscious figures)
- **Shadow journaling** (writing as the rejected parts of self—the anger, the shame, the grief)
- **Therapy** (depth psychology, psychoanalysis, somatic therapy)
- **Plant medicine journeys** (in sacred context, with guidance—descent into the unconscious with Uriel as psychopomp)

**Invocation for Descent:**
> *"Uriel, Guardian of the Underworld, I am ready to descend. I do not ask for comfort—I ask for light in the darkness. Show me what I have buried. Illuminate what I have denied. I am willing to face the shadow. Guide me through."*

### 3. Earth Consecration Rituals

To honor Uriel is to **consecrate the material world**, to recognize the earth as sacred.

**Practices:**
- **Altar work** (create an earth altar with stones, soil, roots, crystals—place offerings)
- **Blessing the body** (anointing with oil, honoring the flesh as temple)
- **Gratitude for material abundance** (food, shelter, health—seeing these as divine gifts, not just "matter")
- **Environmental action** (caring for the earth as a spiritual practice—Uriel as patron of ecological consciousness)

### 4. The Lesser Banishing Ritual of the Pentagram (LBRP)

In ceremonial magic, Uriel is invoked at the **North Quarter** in the LBRP:

**Ritual excerpt:**
> *"On my left hand, Uriel [visualize: a green-robed figure holding a lantern and sheaf of wheat, standing on solid ground, radiating earthy stability]. For about me flames the Pentagram, and within me shines the six-rayed Star."*

This ritual **grounds the practitioner** in the body, in the earth, in presence, while simultaneously opening to the higher archangels (Raphael, Gabriel, Michael).

### 5. Contemplation of the Saturnian Mysteries

To meditate on Uriel is to meditate on **Saturn**—time, limitation, death, wisdom earned through suffering.

**Contemplative questions:**
- *"What have I learned through restriction, through hardship, through the limits of my body and life?"*
- *"Where have I tried to bypass embodiment, to escape into spirit or mind?"*
- *"What is the treasure in the cave I fear to enter?"*
- *"How can I honor my mortality, my aging, my eventual return to earth?"*

### 6. Signs of Uriel's Presence

Uriel does not announce himself with visions of light or celestial music (that is Michael or Gabriel). Uriel's presence is **subtle, grounded, undeniable**:

- A sudden **feeling of being in your body** (presence, groundedness, the sensation of gravity)
- **Prophetic dreams** that reveal hidden truths (not fantastical visions, but clear symbolic messages about shadow or future consequence)
- **Synchronicities involving earth** (finding stones, unexpected encounters with animals, plants growing in unexpected places)
- The experience of **hard truth** that cannot be denied (the lantern illuminating what you would rather not see)
- A deep **sense of the sacred in matter** (the realization that the body, the earth, the physical world are holy)
