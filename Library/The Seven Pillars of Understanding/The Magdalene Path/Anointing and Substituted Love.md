# Anointing and Substituted Love
*The Central Sacrament - Touch, Presence, and Holding the Post*

---
## Core Teaching

**Anointing** is the central sacrament of [[The Magdalene Path]]—the act of consecrating through touch, preparing for transformation, and sealing with love (Bourgeault, Ch. 15). It is:

- **Healing** - The oils soothe and restore
- **Preparation for Death** - Readying the body/soul for passage
- **Consecration** - Setting apart for sacred purpose
- **Love Made Manifest** - Abstract devotion becomes tangible presence

**Substituted Love** is the practice that emerges from anointing—the capacity to **"lay down your life"** for another, not through martyrdom but through **holding the spiritual post** when they cannot. Derived from the theology of Charles Williams (British Christian mystic and novelist), substituted love is the voluntary "bearing of burdens" where one person carries the weight—emotional, physical, or spiritual—of another, so the other is relieved of carrying it (Bourgeault, Ch. 12; Williams, *Descent into Hell*). This is the essence of [[The Bridal Chamber and Sacred Union|conscious partnership]].

**[[Mary Magdalene - Apostle and Beloved|Mary Magdalene]]** embodies both practices—she anoints Jesus' body for burial (Mark 16:1, John 19:39-42) and stands vigil at the cross when others flee (John 19:25). She demonstrates **radical presence** and **substituted love**. Bourgeault suggests that during the three days of death, Magdalene held Jesus's anguish and unresolved human emotions in her own heart so he could travel freely through the underworld to accomplish his cosmic work (Bourgeault, Ch. 12).

## Gospel Foundation

### Jewish Burial Customs

In **1st-century Judaism**, anointing the dead was an act of honor, love, and preparation:

**The Materials:**
- **Myrrh** - Aromatic resin, preservative, symbol of suffering
- **Aloes** - Fragrant wood, purifying
- **Nard** (spikenard) - Expensive perfume, symbol of devotion

#### The Practice
- The body was washed and wrapped in linen
- Spices and oils were placed between the layers of cloth
- The tomb was sealed, and vigil was kept

**The Meaning:** Anointing was intimate, tactile, embodied—the living touching the dead, honoring the transition.

### The Gospel Accounts

**Three Anointing Stories** (conflated by Pope Gregory I in Homily 33, 594 CE, but originally distinct):

**1. The "Sinful Woman" (Luke 7:36-50):**
- An unnamed woman anoints Jesus' feet with perfume, washes them with her tears, dries them with her hair
- Jesus defends her against Simon the Pharisee's judgment
- **The Teaching:** Love shown through touch is more powerful than religious propriety

**2. Mary of Bethany (John 12:1-8):**
- Mary anoints Jesus' feet with expensive nard (worth a year's wages)
- Judas objects to the "waste"
- Jesus responds: **"She bought it so that she might keep it for the day of my burial"**
- **The Teaching:** Anointing prepares for death—it is prophetic, not wasteful

**3. Mary Magdalene at the Tomb (Mark 16:1, John 19:39-42):**
- Mary Magdalene (with other women) comes to anoint Jesus' body after crucifixion
- She finds the tomb empty—the body has been resurrected
- **The Teaching:** Anointing is offered even when the form has dissolved—love persists beyond death

### Chrismation: The Second Sacrament

In **Eastern Orthodox** Christianity, **Chrismation** (anointing with holy oil) is the sacrament that follows baptism—sealing the newly baptized with the Holy Spirit.

**The Oil:** Chrism (μύρον - myron) - blessed oil mixed with fragrant spices

**The Act:** The priest anoints the forehead, eyes, ears, nostrils, lips, chest, hands, and feet—saying:
> "The seal of the gift of the Holy Spirit."

**The Teaching:** Anointing is not just for the dead—it is for the **living**, sealing them for transformation.

### Unction: Anointing the Sick and Dying

In both **Catholic** and **Orthodox** traditions, **Extreme Unction** (anointing of the sick/dying) prepares the soul for passage.

#### The Practice
- The priest anoints the dying person's senses (forehead, hands)
- Prayers for healing, forgiveness, safe passage

**The Teaching:** Anointing creates **safe passage** through the bardo realms (the intermediate states between death and rebirth).

## Mystical Depth

### Touch as Transmission

**The Teaching:** Anointing is not symbolic—it is **energetic**. Touch transmits:
- **Presence** - "I am here with you"
- **Love** - "You are not alone"
- **Consecration** - "You are sacred"

**The Science:** Research on touch shows:
- Reduces cortisol (stress hormone)
- Increases oxytocin (bonding hormone)
- Activates parasympathetic nervous system (rest/digest)

**The Mysticism:** Touch is the **transfer of pneuma** (spirit/breath)—like the kiss in the Gospel of Philip, anointing shares essence.

### Preparation for Death (Literal and Metaphoric)

**Literal Death:**

When someone is dying, anointing:
- **Eases the passage** - Reduces fear, creates calm
- **Seals the soul** - Protects during the journey through bardo realms
- **Honors the body** - Treats the vehicle with reverence as it is released

**Metaphoric Death:**

During [[The Eight Boughs of Ascent|the Lower Astral]] or [[The Eight Boughs of Ascent|the Abyss]], the soul undergoes **ego death**—anointing supports this:
- The beloved anoints you as the false self dies
- The touch says: "You are safe. I am holding the space."
- This creates **container** for transformation

### The Seven Senses

Traditional anointing touches **seven points**—corresponding to the senses and chakras:

1. **Forehead (Third Eye)** - Vision, insight, clarity
2. **Eyes** - Outer seeing, witnessing
3. **Ears** - Hearing, listening, receptivity
4. **Nostrils** - Smell, breath, life force
5. **Lips** - Speech, truth, kiss
6. **Chest (Heart)** - Love, compassion, feeling
7. **Hands and Feet** - Action, service, embodiment

**The Practice:** Slowly, reverently anoint each point—pausing to **feel** the contact, the presence, the love.

## Substituted Love: Holding the Post

### The Teaching (Cynthia Bourgeault via Charles Williams)

**Substituted Love** is the practice of **"laying down your life"** for another—not martyrdom, but **exchanging places**. Charles Williams called this the "theology of romantic love" and described it as "mystical substitution" (Bourgeault, Ch. 12). In his novels (*Descent into Hell*, *All Hallows' Eve*), Williams depicts characters literally taking on another's fear, neurosis, or pain through a mystical exchange.

**The Mechanic:**
1. When the beloved is in pain, fear, or confusion, you **do not** fix, advise, or rescue
2. You **stand in their place**—energetically, you imagine yourself as them, feeling what they feel
3. You **hold the truth** of their wholeness when they cannot see it themselves
4. This creates a **"third term"** or **Abler Soul**—a unified field of consciousness greater than either individual

**The Abler Soul:** This term comes from John Donne's poem "A Valediction: Forbidding Mourning." The Abler Soul is not just an energy field but a "subtle body" or "wedding garment" generated by the love between two people. While the partners are alive, it remains hidden, but upon death, it emerges as a stable, energetic bridge between the realms, allowing lovers to remain "inter-assured of the mind" across the divide of life and death (Bourgeault, Ch. 12; Donne).

**The Requirement:** You must be **Single** (whole, not seeking completion) to practice substituted love. If you are not whole, you will collapse into codependency (seeking to save them so you feel valuable).

### The Practice

**Step 1: Presence**

When your partner/friend/child is suffering:
- **Stop** - Put down your phone, your task, your agenda
- **Turn toward them** - Physically, emotionally, energetically
- **Breathe** - Ground yourself in your body

**Step 2: Stand in Their Place**

Close your eyes (or soften your gaze) and **imagine**:
- You are them
- You feel what they feel (the fear, the pain, the confusion)
- You see from their perspective

**Do NOT:**
- Think about how to fix it
- Judge them for feeling this way
- Try to make it go away

**Step 3: Hold the Truth**

While standing in their place, **simultaneously** hold the truth:
- They are whole (even if they can't see it)
- They are loved (even if they don't feel it)
- This will pass (even if it feels eternal)

**The Paradox:** You feel their pain (full empathy) **while** holding their wholeness (unshakeable faith). This is the **tension** that creates the Abler Soul.

**Step 4: Transmit**

Sometimes with words, often without—transmit the truth:
- "I am here."
- "You are not alone."
- "This is hard, and you are strong enough."

Or simply: Touch. Anoint. Hold.

### The Bardo: Safe Passage

In **Tibetan Buddhism**, the **bardo** (བར་དོ) is the intermediate state between death and rebirth—a liminal zone where the soul is vulnerable.

**The Teaching:** Substituted love provides **safe passage** through the bardo—whether literal death or metaphoric (ego death, crisis, transformation).

#### How It Works

When someone is in the bardo:
- They are disoriented, frightened, unable to navigate alone
- The **Abler Soul** (the one who holds the post) acts as **guide, anchor, light**
- Their presence says: "Follow me. I know the way. You are safe."

**The Application:**
- A parent holding vigil while a child goes through addiction recovery
- A partner standing steady while the other crosses [[The Eight Boughs of Ascent|the Abyss]]
- A friend anointing someone as they die

## Anointing in Partnership

### Eucharistic Anointing

In [[The Bridal Chamber and Sacred Union|the Bridal Chamber]], anointing becomes **eucharistic**—"This is my body, given for you."

#### The Practice

Before or during sexual union:
- One partner anoints the other with oil (olive, almond, or sacred blends)
- Touch **slowly, reverently**—forehead, heart, hands, feet, genitals
- Speak (aloud or silently): *"I consecrate you. I honor you. You are sacred."*

**The Result:**

The beloved is **seen**—not as object of desire, but as **divine form**. Sexual union becomes sacrament.

### The Shadow: Savior Complex

**The Danger:** Using substituted love to feel **needed** or **superior**.

**Example:** "I'm the only one who can save them. Without me, they'd fall apart."

**Reality:** This is **not** substituted love—it is **codependency**. You are using their suffering to fill your void.

**The Test:**

Ask: *"Can I hold the post and still let them fall?"*

If you **need** them to succeed (to validate your worth), you are not practicing substituted love—you are controlling.

**The Antidote:** Achieve **singleness** first. Be whole. Then, you can stand in their place without needing them to stay.

At [[The Eight Boughs of Ascent|the Base]], anointing is **grounding**—bringing presence into the physical body.

### Lower Astral: Purification

At [[The Eight Boughs of Ascent|the Lower Astral]], anointing supports the **stripping away** of false self.

At [[The Eight Boughs of Ascent|the Abyss]], anointing creates **safe passage** through ego death.

**The Practice:** The beloved anoints you as you **fall**—their touch is the anchor, the thread back to embodiment.

At [[The Eight Boughs of Ascent|the Zenith]], anointing is **consecration**—sealing the soul for Cosmic Service.

**The Practice:** Anoint the crown (fontanelle)—the place where spirit enters and exits the body.

## Practical Anointing

### Creating Sacred Oil

**Base Oil:**
- Olive oil (traditional, stable)
- Almond oil (light, nourishing)
- Jojoba oil (long-lasting, skin-friendly)

**Essential Oils (Optional):**
- **Myrrh** - Death, transformation, suffering
- **Frankincense** - Elevation, prayer, sanctification
- **Rose** - Love, heart-opening, devotion
- **Sandalwood** - Grounding, presence, sacred sexuality

**The Blessing:**

Hold the oil and pray (your own words or traditional):
> "May this oil be consecrated. May it carry healing, love, and presence. May it serve the highest good."

### The Seven-Point Anointing

**Forehead:**
> "May your vision be clear."

**Eyes:**
> "May you see truth."

**Ears:**
> "May you hear the Divine."

**Nostrils:**
> "May you breathe life."

**Lips:**
> "May you speak wisdom."

**Heart:**
> "May you love freely."

**Hands/Feet:**
> "May you serve and walk in beauty."
